About

WCPA — the World Cup Prediction Album — is a statistical companion to the 2026 FIFA World Cup. A hand-built model — international Elo plus a time-decayed Dixon-Coles goals model, blended and calibrated — is backtested on more than 49,000 internationals dating to 1872, then used to call every match, estimate each nation's title odds, and project the full wallchart. It's laid out like a retro sticker album because football should be fun.

It's an independent project by Samuel Bowey, built in Australia. The whole engine is pure Python over a PostgreSQL database; the numbers update from a live feed of results and news as the tournament unfolds. How the model is measured (held-out accuracy, calibration) is shown openly on the site's “Method” page — this is published as data, with its track record visible, not as a tipping service.
